How to Clean & Waterproof Your Hiking Boots

Dirty boots aren't just a badge of honor; they're a liability. When mud dries on your leather or synthetic boots, it sucks away the moisture, leading to cracks and premature failure of the Gore-Tex membrane.

I'm Xander Hayes. After a muddy season in the Cascades, I’ve perfected a cleaning ritual that will double the life of your Lowa or Garmont boots. Here is the step-by-step process.

Step 1: The Dry Brush

Remove the laces. Use a medium-stiff brush to knock off all dried mud and dust. Do not skip this; adding water to a muddy boot just creates a mess that pushes grit deeper into the seams.

Pro Tip: Never use a metal wire brush on technical footwear. It will shred the stitching and ruin the waterproof coating.

Step 2: Deep Cleaning

Use a specialized boot cleaner (like Nikwax) and cool water. Gently scrub the entire boot. Avoid using regular dish soap, as the harsh chemicals can strip the natural oils from the leather.

Step 3: Re-Waterproofing (DWR)

While the boots are still damp, apply your waterproofing treatment. This allows the formula to follow the water into the pores of the material, ensuring a deeper bond. Focus on the flex points and seams.

Step 4: The Slow Dry

Stuff your boots with newspaper and let them dry naturally. NEVER put them near a heater or campfire. Intense heat makes the glue brittle and causes the leather to shrink and crack.

Take care of your boots, and they will take care of your feet for hundreds of miles. See you on the trail!
